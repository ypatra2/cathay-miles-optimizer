# Best Credit Card for Uber Rides in Hong Kong (Asia Miles Focus)

## Executive Summary

Among HSBC Red Mastercard, HSBC EveryMile Visa, HSBC Visa Signature and Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard, the HSBC Red Mastercard is generally the best card to use for Uber rides in Hong Kong to maximise Asia Miles, as long as: (1) the ride is charged as an online transaction (which it typically is), and (2) the spend still falls within the monthly HKD10,000 online‑spend cap for 4% RewardCash on the Red card.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5]
Under these conditions, Red delivers an effective earn rate of about HKD2.5 per Asia Mile on Uber rides, better than HSBC EveryMile (HKD5 per mile on non‑designated merchants), Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard (HKD6 per mile on local/online non‑bonus spend) and HSBC Visa Signature (≈HKD2.78 per mile even in its boosted overseas category).[^6][^2][^7][^8][^9][^10]

Separately, by linking your Cathay membership inside the Uber app and choosing Cathay as the earning partner, you earn fixed Asia Miles per Uber Taxi ride (20 miles per local trip; 40 miles per airport trip) on top of what any credit card generates, so this should be enabled regardless of which card you use.[^11][^12][^13][^14]

***

## 1. How Uber Rides in Hong Kong Post on Credit Cards

### 1.1 Online and cross‑border nature of Uber HK transactions

Uber rides in Hong Kong are always paid in‑app by card; there is no cash option, and payment clears via the registered card.[^15][^16][^17]
Community reports and fee guides for HSBC credit cards show that Uber charges, although displayed in HKD, are processed by overseas entities (e.g., Uber BV in the Netherlands or regional hubs), triggering “cross‑border” or “overseas HKD” surcharges on many Hong Kong‑issued cards.[^18][^19][^20]
HSBC’s cross‑border fee examples explicitly list Uber among merchants where HKD charges incur a 1% “DCC Fee – non‑HK Merchant”, confirming that Uber HK transactions are treated as overseas‑processed e‑commerce charges even though the ride occurs locally.[^19][^21]

### 1.2 Whether Uber counts as “online spending”

The HSBC Red Credit Card awards 4% RewardCash on the first HKD10,000 of online purchases each calendar month, with 0.4% thereafter, irrespective of currency, as long as the transaction is recognised as online.[^3][^4][^22][^1]
Hong Kong credit‑card discussion forums note that Uber rides do earn the 4% online RewardCash on HSBC Red, with users explicitly mentioning “HSBC Red 4%” when asked which cards can earn rebates or points on Uber rides, implying that local issuers treat Uber HK charges as online transactions.[^5]
E‑commerce fee write‑ups similarly include Uber alongside other app‑based and foreign‑processed online merchants (Netflix, Airbnb, Agoda, Apple) for HSBC cards, further supporting the classification of Uber as online spend for HSBC Red’s purposes.[^20][^19]

## 2. Asia Miles Earning Mechanics of Each Card

### 2.1 HSBC Red Mastercard

HSBC Red is a RewardCash (cashback/points) card whose RewardCash can be converted into Asia Miles at a fixed rate.[^23][^1]
Independent card reviews and comparison sites consistently state that when you use Red for online spending, you effectively earn Asia Miles at around HKD2.5 per mile, assuming you convert RewardCash to Asia Miles.[^2][^4][^3]
This figure comes from:

- 4% RewardCash on online spending on the first HKD10,000 per calendar month (i.e., HKD100 spend generates HKD4 RewardCash).[^4][^1][^3]
- RewardCash‑to‑Asia‑Miles conversion rate on Red such that this 4% rebate equates to roughly HKD2.5 of spend per Asia Mile.[^2][^3]

For non‑online or post‑cap online spending, Red earns only 0.4% RewardCash, which corresponds to an effective Asia Miles earn rate of around HKD25 per mile (i.e., 10 times worse than the 4% online rate).[^3][^23]

### 2.2 HSBC EveryMile Visa

HSBC EveryMile also earns RewardCash, but it is marketed as a travel‑focused miles card because RewardCash converts to miles at a better rate than on other HSBC cards.[^24][^25]
According to HSBC and independent comparisons:

- Designated everyday and travel spend (selected cafés/light meals, local and cross‑border transportation, and travel services) earns RewardCash at a rate equivalent to HKD2 spent = 1 mile.[^7][^8][^26][^27][^28][^24]
- General local and overseas purchases earn at a lower rate of 1% RewardCash, equivalent to HKD5 spent = 1 mile.[^8][^26][^27][^7]

HSBC publishes a “Designated Everyday & Travel Spend Merchants” list for EveryMile; the local transportation and taxi‑app section includes public transport operators (KMB, Citybus, MTR, trams) and certain taxi apps (Amigo, Big Bee, Dash, Joie, SynCab), but Uber is not listed.[^26][^27][^29][^28]
Hence Uber rides are treated as general local (or overseas HKD) purchases on EveryMile, earning at the weaker HKD5 = 1 mile rate.[^28][^7][^8][^26]

### 2.3 HSBC Visa Signature

HSBC Visa Signature participates in the same RewardCash programme and can be boosted via “Red Hot Rewards of Your Choice”, where you allocate a 5× RewardCash multiplier to a preferred category such as Dining or Overseas & Mainland China Spending.[^30][^31][^23]
Comparison tools that translate cashback to miles show that, when fully optimised, Visa Signature yields effective Asia Miles earn rates roughly as follows:[^10][^2]

- Local (non‑boosted) spend: around HKD2.78 per mile for the chosen boosted category; worse for un‑boosted categories.
- Overseas spending category optimised: about 3.6% cashback equivalent, which when converted at 1 RewardCash = 10 Asia Miles gives ≈HKD2.78 per mile.[^10][^2]

Visa Signature does not have any special bonus sub‑category for app‑based ride‑hailing or taxis; Uber rides are simply classified according to whether the issuer treats them as overseas spend (due to overseas processing) or general local spend.[^30][^19][^20]

### 2.4 Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard

The Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard is a direct‑earn Asia Miles card (no conversion step): eligible spending posts Asia Miles directly to your Cathay account.[^9][^32][^6]
Current product terms and comparison tables describe its earn structure broadly as:[^33][^34][^32][^6][^9]

- HKD2 = 1 Asia Mile on Cathay and HK Express transactions (tickets, duty‑free, Cathay Holidays), subject to tier conditions and caps.[^6][^33][^9]
- Between HKD2 and HKD4 = 1 Asia Mile on overseas spending, dining and hotels depending on your banking segment (Priority Private, Priority Banking or standard).[^34][^32][^9][^6]
- HKD6 = 1 Asia Mile on other non‑bonus spend posted in Hong Kong dollars, including general online and local transactions.[^32][^9][^34][^6]

Unless Uber is explicitly recognised as a dining/food‑delivery or overseas category transaction under SC Cathay’s selected categories T&Cs, Uber rides in Hong Kong will generally fall into “online and other categories that are posted in Hong Kong dollars”, earning HKD6 = 1 mile.[^35][^34][^32]

## 4. Interaction with Uber Taxi x Cathay Asia Miles Partnership

### 4.1 Direct Uber‑Cathay earning

Uber Taxi and Cathay launched a partnership under which Cathay members earn fixed Asia Miles per ride in Hong Kong once they link accounts and select Cathay as the earning partner in the Uber app.[^36][^12][^13][^14][^37][^11]
The current published earn rates are:

- 20 Asia Miles on every Uber Taxi ride in Hong Kong (non‑airport).[^12][^13][^14][^11]
- 40 Asia Miles on Uber Taxi rides to and from Hong Kong International Airport.[^13][^14][^11][^12]
- Occasional promotional bonuses (e.g., 500 extra miles for first‑time Uber users or CNY bonus pools) on top of the base 20/40 miles.[^14][^37][^11][^12]

These miles are earned independently of the credit‑card rewards; any eligible card can be used as the payment method in Uber Taxi while still earning the fixed 20/40 Asia Miles per qualifying trip.[^11][^13][^14]

### 4.2 Combined effect with your credit card

For Uber Taxi rides (as distinct from UberX/private‑hire), you can therefore earn two layers of Asia Miles simultaneously:

- Fixed per‑ride miles from the Uber‑Cathay partnership after linking accounts (20 or 40 per ride).[^12][^13][^14][^11]
- Variable miles from your credit card, based on the HKD fare and the card’s earn rate (e.g., Red at ≈HKD2.5 per mile on online transactions under cap).[^4][^1][^2][^3][^5]

Since the partner miles do not depend on card choice, the optimal strategy is to choose the card with the best HKD‑per‑mile earn rate (usually HSBC Red) as the payment method, while also ensuring the Uber‑Cathay linkage is active so you collect the per‑ride miles on top.[^13][^14][^1][^6][^2][^3][^4][^11][^12]

## 5. Edge Cases and When Another Card Might Be Competitive

### 5.1 If you have exhausted HSBC Red’s monthly HKD10,000 online cap

Once your cumulative online spending on HSBC Red exceeds HKD10,000 in a calendar month, further online transactions—including Uber rides—drop from 4% RewardCash to 0.4% RewardCash, which equates to about HKD25 per mile.[^22][^23][^1][^3]
In this situation, Red becomes the worst card for Uber, and you should switch to alternatives:

- If you want pure Asia Miles: HSBC EveryMile (HKD5 per mile on non‑designated merchants) or SC Cathay (HKD6 per mile on HKD spend; HKD4 per mile or better if Uber is treated as overseas) will both dramatically outperform Red’s HKD25 per mile.[^7][^8][^33][^26][^27][^34][^32][^6][^9]
- If your HSBC Visa Signature has Overseas & Mainland China Spending selected and the issuer is tagging Uber as overseas, Visa Signature at ≈HKD2.78 per mile may then become your best choice among the remaining three.[^19][^20][^2][^30][^10]

### 5.2 If HSBC treats Uber as “overseas” but not “online” for some reason

Published data and user reports strongly suggest Uber HK transactions are both overseas‑processed and online, but if a future system or rule change caused HSBC to stop tagging Uber as e‑commerce, Red would no longer receive 4% RewardCash on Uber rides.[^20][^5][^19]
In that hypothetical scenario:

- HSBC EveryMile would likely still give HKD5 per mile on Uber (general purchase), unless Uber were later added to the designated transport/taxi‑app list.[^8][^26][^27][^28][^7]
- HSBC Visa Signature, with Overseas & Mainland China selected, could offer ≈HKD2.78 per mile if Uber is recognised as overseas spend, making it the new top option among your HSBC cards.[^2][^30][^10][^19]
- SC Cathay might earn at HKD4 per mile or better if the charge is treated as overseas (non‑HKD) on your specific card variant, which would be competitive with, or better than, EveryMile but still worse than Visa Signature at 2.78.[^34][^35][^6][^9]

As of the most recent factsheets and third‑party analyses, however, there is no indication that Uber is excluded from HSBC Red’s online definition, and community datapoints confirm 4% RewardCash is being earned on Uber, so this remains an edge case rather than the base expectation.[^1][^3][^4][^5][^19]

### 5.3 When to prefer HSBC EveryMile despite weaker Uber earn rate

While EveryMile is weaker than Red for Uber specifically (HKD5 per mile vs HKD2.5 per mile), it may still be rational to route some Uber spend to EveryMile if:

- You are working toward EveryMile’s welcome offer thresholds or other spend‑based promotions, and moving Uber rides there helps hit the target without sacrificing more valuable bonus categories elsewhere.[^25][^24][^26][^7][^8]
- Your Red card’s online‑spend cap is consistently maxed out by higher‑value online transactions (e.g., large flight and hotel bookings), and you prefer to reserve Red’s 4% quota for those.[^22][^3][^4][^1]

In such cases, the opportunity‑cost framing may justify taking the lower earn rate on Uber while preserving Red’s cap for other online transactions that cannot easily be moved.

## 6. Practical Recommendations for Your Four Cards

Based on the current HSBC and Standard Chartered factsheets, plus third‑party analyses and user reports, the following priority order is recommended for Uber rides in Hong Kong if maximising Asia Miles is your only objective:

1. **Primary card for Uber (most months): HSBC Red Mastercard**  
   Use Red for Uber rides as long as (a) your monthly online spending on Red is still under HKD10,000, and (b) your statement data confirms Uber is being treated as online (i.e., earning 4% RewardCash).[^3][^4][^5][^22][^1][^2]
   This should give you around HKD2.5 per Asia Mile on Uber fares—better than all three of your other cards for this category in normal circumstances.[^4][^2][^3]

2. **Secondary card if Red cap is used up: HSBC Visa Signature (if optimised for overseas)**  
   If your statements show Uber transactions tagged as overseas and you have selected “Overseas & Mainland China Spending” under Red Hot Rewards of Your Choice, route Uber spend to Visa Signature next for an effective ≈HKD2.78 per mile, which is still materially better than EveryMile’s HKD5 per mile and SC Cathay’s HKD6 per mile on HKD transactions.[^23][^30][^10][^19][^20][^2]

3. **Third choice: HSBC EveryMile Visa**  
   If Red is capped and Visa Signature is not optimised for overseas (or Uber is not flagged as overseas on that card), then HSBC EveryMile at HKD5 per mile on general purchases is your next‑best option for Uber.[^26][^27][^28][^7][^8]
   Do not expect the HKD2 per mile transport/taxi‑app rate unless HSBC updates the designated merchant list to include Uber.

4. **Last resort for Uber: Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard**  
   For Uber HK rides coded as local/online HKD transactions, SC Cathay typically earns only HKD6 per mile, which is strictly worse than EveryMile’s HKD5 per mile and far behind Red and Visa Signature in their strong categories.[^33][^32][^6][^9][^34]
   SC Cathay is therefore better reserved for categories where it shines (Cathay/HK Express tickets, dining/hotels, or pure simplicity when you want miles credited directly without manual conversion) rather than for Uber.

5. **Always link the Uber‑Cathay partnership regardless of card**  
   Independently of which card you use, link your Cathay account within the Uber app and set Cathay as the earning partner so you receive the fixed 20/40 miles per Uber Taxi ride on top of your card miles.[^37][^14][^11][^12][^13]

## 7. What You Should Monitor in Your Own Statements

Because banks can and do update merchant mappings and category logic over time, the final step is to validate the above in your own data:

- Check HSBC Red e‑statements to ensure Uber transactions are consistently appearing under online spending and earning 4% RewardCash; if they are, Red remains your clear best choice for Uber until the HKD10,000 cap is reached.[^5][^1][^3][^4]
- On EveryMile, confirm that Uber rides are earning at the general 1% RewardCash (HKD5 per mile) rather than any higher transport rate; unless HSBC adds Uber to the designated list, this pattern should hold.[^27][^28][^7][^8][^26]
- On HSBC Visa Signature and SC Cathay, check whether Uber is categorised as overseas or simply general HKD spending; this affects whether their overseas‑spend earn rates ever make them competitive with Red for Uber.[^6][^30][^34][^10][^19][^20]

Tracking a few months of real‑world data will confirm how your specific card setups interact with Uber, but given current factsheets and public datapoints, HSBC Red Mastercard is the most suitable of your four cards for Uber rides in Hong Kong to maximise Asia Miles in most realistic scenarios.[^7][^8][^33][^9][^26][^27][^34][^10][^1][^6][^2][^3][^4][^5]
